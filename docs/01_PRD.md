# Product Requirements Document

**Project:** MISO

**Version:** 1.0

**Owner:** Product Team

**Status:** Living Document

---

# Overview

MISO is a premium Pan Asian restaurant platform designed to transform dining into an immersive cultural experience. Unlike conventional food delivery applications that prioritize speed and discounts, MISO focuses on discovery, hospitality, storytelling, reservations, and intelligent personalization while maintaining a world class user experience.

The platform combines restaurant discovery, reservations, online ordering, digital dining, loyalty programs, AI powered recommendations, and cultural exploration into a unified ecosystem that serves diners, restaurant owners, and administrators through one connected product.

---

# Product Objective

The primary objective of MISO is to remove friction from every stage of the dining journey while making every interaction memorable.

The product should help users discover authentic restaurants, understand cuisines, reserve tables effortlessly, customize meals, order confidently, receive intelligent recommendations, and build long term relationships with restaurants through premium digital experiences.

Every feature should strengthen trust, simplicity, and emotional engagement.

---

# Problem Statement

Current restaurant applications focus primarily on transactions.

Users are overwhelmed by advertisements, excessive promotions, inconsistent interfaces, poor restaurant discovery, and limited personalization. Restaurant owners struggle to communicate their identity beyond menus and pricing, while customers rarely experience the stories, craftsmanship, and culture behind the food they consume.

MISO solves these problems by creating a platform where restaurants become destinations rather than listings, food becomes storytelling rather than inventory, and technology quietly enhances every interaction without becoming intrusive.

---

# Success Criteria

The product will be considered successful when users consistently choose MISO as their preferred platform for discovering restaurants, making reservations, placing orders, and exploring Asian cuisine.

Success should be measured through user retention, completed reservations, repeat orders, restaurant satisfaction, average session duration, customer engagement, recommendation accuracy, loyalty participation, and overall user happiness rather than downloads alone.

---

# Product Scope

The first version of MISO includes restaurant discovery, advanced search, personalized recommendations, restaurant profiles, menu browsing, dish customization, online ordering, reservations, QR dining, secure payments, loyalty rewards, AI powered assistance, profile management, restaurant management, and administrative operations.

Future versions may expand into culinary tourism, chef experiences, food festivals, subscription memberships, cooking classes, premium concierge services, and international expansion.

---

# User Roles

The platform serves four primary user groups.

Customers explore restaurants, reserve tables, place orders, manage rewards, and discover cuisines.

Restaurant Owners manage menus, reservations, inventory, promotions, staff operations, customer engagement, and analytics.

Administrators oversee platform operations, restaurant verification, moderation, support, analytics, security, and business performance.

Delivery Partners receive assigned deliveries, navigate routes, update delivery status, and complete customer fulfillment.

Each role should receive a dedicated interface tailored to its responsibilities while sharing one unified design language.

---

# Core Functional Requirements

Users must be able to create secure accounts, authenticate through multiple providers, recover forgotten credentials, and manage personal preferences.

Users must discover restaurants through intelligent search, curated collections, cuisine categories, nearby recommendations, and personalized suggestions.

Restaurant profiles must provide immersive photography, detailed descriptions, menus, chef stories, operating hours, reviews, ratings, contact information, directions, and reservation availability.

Menus must support categories, customization, dietary preferences, allergens, nutritional information, portion selection, spice levels, add ons, and chef recommendations.

Users must place delivery, pickup, or dine in orders using secure payment methods while tracking order progress in real time.

The reservation system must support table booking, guest management, seating preferences, special occasions, reminders, confirmations, and modifications.

QR dining must allow customers to scan restaurant tables, browse digital menus, order food, request assistance, and complete payments without waiting for staff.

Profiles must store addresses, payment methods, order history, reservation history, favorites, rewards, achievements, preferences, and account settings.

Restaurant owners must manage menus, reservations, inventory, operating hours, pricing, promotions, customer feedback, and business analytics.

Administrators must verify restaurants, moderate content, resolve disputes, monitor system health, manage users, publish announcements, and generate reports.

---

# Non Functional Requirements

The application should load quickly under normal network conditions and maintain smooth interactions throughout every screen.

Animations should remain fluid and responsive without impacting usability.

The interface must support accessibility features including scalable typography, screen readers, sufficient contrast ratios, and intuitive navigation.

Authentication, payments, and user data must follow industry standard security practices with encrypted communication and secure storage.

The platform should support horizontal scaling to accommodate increasing numbers of restaurants and users without architectural changes.

Offline handling should gracefully preserve user progress whenever network connectivity is temporarily unavailable.

---

# Business Rules

Only verified restaurants may publish menus and accept reservations.

Reservations may require deposits depending on restaurant policy.

Orders cannot be modified once preparation has begun unless approved by the restaurant.

Reward points are earned only after successful order completion or reservation attendance.

Refund eligibility depends on cancellation timing, payment method, and restaurant policy.

Restaurant ratings become publicly visible only after verified customer visits or completed orders.

Every business rule should prioritize transparency and fairness for both customers and restaurant partners.

---

# User Journey

The customer journey begins with discovery. Users explore cuisines, restaurants, and recommendations before selecting a restaurant that matches their preferences. They browse menus, customize dishes, reserve tables or place orders, complete secure payments, receive real time updates, enjoy their dining experience, leave reviews, earn rewards, and return with increasingly personalized recommendations.

Restaurant owners receive reservations and orders, manage operational workflows, prepare meals, update statuses, analyze business performance, respond to customer feedback, and continuously improve their service through platform insights.

Administrators ensure quality, security, compliance, operational reliability, and ecosystem growth across the entire platform.

---

# Feature Prioritization

The first release focuses on delivering a premium and reliable dining experience through restaurant discovery, reservations, ordering, payments, QR dining, user profiles, restaurant dashboards, and administrative tools.

Artificial intelligence, advanced personalization, loyalty systems, cultural storytelling, analytics, and premium experiences strengthen the platform without distracting from the core dining journey.

Every feature should support the product vision rather than compete for user attention.

---

# Assumptions

Users expect intuitive navigation, trustworthy recommendations, secure payments, and premium design.

Restaurants expect operational simplicity, customer growth, and actionable insights.

The platform should remain flexible enough to support regional differences while maintaining one consistent product experience.

Technology should remain invisible, allowing hospitality and food to become the primary focus.

---

# Risks

Inconsistent restaurant data may reduce user trust.

Overly complex interfaces may decrease engagement.

Poor recommendation quality may negatively affect discovery.

Operational delays from restaurant partners may impact customer satisfaction.

Rapid feature expansion without maintaining design consistency may weaken the product identity.

Every major product decision should reduce one or more of these risks.

---

# Dependencies

The success of MISO depends upon reliable authentication services, payment gateways, location services, mapping providers, notification infrastructure, analytics platforms, cloud storage, database services, restaurant partnerships, and content moderation systems.

These dependencies should be designed with redundancy and scalability to ensure long term reliability.

---

# Out of Scope

The initial release will not include grocery delivery, social networking, influencer content creation, live streaming, cryptocurrency payments, table management hardware integration, franchise ERP systems, or non restaurant commerce.

These opportunities may be evaluated in future product iterations once the core dining experience has matured.

---

# Acceptance Criteria

Every feature released within MISO must satisfy four conditions before reaching production.

It must solve a genuine user problem.

It must align with the product vision.

It must integrate seamlessly into the existing user experience.

It must maintain the premium quality standard established by the platform.

If any feature fails one of these conditions, it should be redesigned or postponed.

---

# Definition of Success

MISO succeeds when users stop thinking of it as a food ordering application and begin thinking of it as their personal gateway to discovering, experiencing, and remembering the best of Asian cuisine.

Every release should move the platform closer to that vision while preserving simplicity, elegance, reliability, and authenticity.
